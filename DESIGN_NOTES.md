# Design Notes

## Brand

Brand name: Velora

Velora should read as a premium contemporary commerce brand with a calm, assured tone. The visual language should feel composed and expensive without relying on loud luxury cues.

## Visual Principles

- Use warm neutrals, soft metallic accents, and restrained contrast instead of harsh monochrome dominance.
- Favor large editorial typography for section entry and compact, precise typography for commerce detail.
- Keep surfaces layered and tactile through borders, transparency, blur, and tonal depth.
- Avoid generic marketplace clutter; every dense section needs a clear primary action and visible rhythm.

## UX Principles

- Storytelling should support shopping, not compete with it.
- Users should always understand what to do next on product, cart, checkout, and account pages.
- Post-purchase pages must feel as polished as conversion pages because they shape trust and retention.
- Empty and edge states should preserve premium tone rather than look like development leftovers.

## Product Merchandising Principles

- Merchandise by lifestyle and use case, not only by technical spec.
- Product names, descriptions, and bundles should feel brand-authored and believable.
- Recommendations should support gifting, upgrades, and cross-category system building.
- Promotional messaging should remain measured; trust and curation matter more than aggressive discount language.

## Content Tone

- English: precise, premium, and understated.
- Traditional Chinese: native, elegant, and commercially natural for Hong Kong users.
- Never expose internal showcase intent in user-facing copy.

## Motion And Interaction

- Motion should be subtle and useful: reveal, focus, confirmation, and context transitions.
- Hover and pressed states should communicate material quality rather than novelty.
- Feedback should be immediate on add-to-cart, reorder, checkout, and form submission flows.

## Layout Guidance

- Home, category, and editorial pages can be spacious and atmospheric.
- PDP, cart, checkout, and account pages should remain dense enough for efficiency without losing polish.
- On mobile, prioritize action visibility, readable summaries, and short interaction paths.
